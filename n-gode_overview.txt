Core:  
A formal evaluation‑engine framework that detects when an agent, system, or organization is pursuing a negative or adversarial goal (e.g., destroying an enemy, capturing a criminal) in a way that violates or undermines higher‑order positive goals (e.g., protecting people, preserving life, long‑term survival).  

Below is a full, modular, DAG‑structured evaluation engine—compatible with your existing architectures (IIP‑IOSEFE, TBTF, FRA‑01, linkage engines)—that identifies goal‑priority violations, negative‑goal overreach, and mission drift toward harm.

---

⚖️ NEGATIVE‑GOAL OVERRIDE DETECTION ENGINE (NGODE)

A DAG‑structured evaluation engine that determines whether an actor’s behavior, plan, or system is adhering to negative goals in violation of primary positive aims.

---

1. 🧭 Foundational Principle
Negative goals (e.g., neutralize threat, capture target, destroy asset) are instrumental.  
Positive goals (e.g., protect civilians, preserve life, ensure long‑term stability) are terminal.

The engine evaluates whether the system is:
- Over‑optimizing for the instrumental goal  
- Undermining or violating the terminal goal  
- Failing to detect conflicts between the two  
- Rationalizing harmful actions under the guise of necessity  

This is essentially a goal‑hierarchy integrity checker.

---

2. 🧩 Top‑Level DAG Structure

Each bullet begins with a Guided Link, as required.

2.1 Goal Hierarchy Extraction
- Parse explicit goals, implicit goals, constraints, and mission statements  
- Identify primary (positive) vs secondary (negative) goals  
- Detect missing or ambiguous goal declarations  

2.2 Positive‑Goal Integrity Model
- Encode survival, safety, rights, and long‑term welfare as protected invariants  
- Assign weights and non‑negotiable constraints  
- Build a “cannot violate” rule set  

2.3 Negative‑Goal Activation Monitor
- Detect when negative goals become dominant  
- Track escalation patterns  
- Identify emotional, political, or situational triggers  

2.4 Conflict Detection Layer
- Compare predicted outcomes of negative‑goal pursuit vs positive‑goal preservation  
- Identify contradictions, violations, or tradeoff inversions  
- Flag “instrumental overreach”  

2.5 Consequence Simulation Engine
- Multi‑trajectory simulation  
- Harm‑minimization scoring  
- Civilian‑impact modeling  
- Long‑term stability modeling  

2.6 Violation Severity Scoring
- Quantify degree of positive‑goal violation  
- Score based on magnitude, duration, reversibility, and systemic impact  

2.7 Override Detection Verdict
- “No violation”  
- “Minor drift”  
- “Critical override”  
- “Catastrophic inversion”  

2.8 Remediation & Re‑alignment Module
- Suggest alternative actions  
- Re‑prioritize positive goals  
- Re‑establish constraints  
- Provide safe‑path recommendations  

---

3. 🔍 Key Analytical Concepts

3.1 Goal‑Priority Inversion
Occurs when:
- Negative goal becomes the de facto primary goal  
- Positive goal becomes conditional or ignored  
- System rationalizes harm as “necessary”  

3.2 Instrumental Overreach
The system:
- Pursues the negative goal beyond what is required  
- Expands scope (mission creep)  
- Applies excessive force or unnecessary harm  

3.3 Positive‑Goal Erosion
Slow degradation of:
- Safety constraints  
- Ethical boundaries  
- Civilian protections  
- Long‑term welfare considerations  

3.4 Catastrophic Inversion
The system now:
- Causes more harm than the threat it was meant to stop  
- Endangers the very population it was meant to protect

4. 🧠 Mathematical Core (High‑Level)

4.1 Goal Priority Function
\[
P(g) = wg \cdot Ig \cdot C_g
\]
Where:  
- \( w_g \) = normative weight  
- \( I_g \) = importance in mission hierarchy  
- \( C_g \) = constraint strength  

4.2 Violation Score
\[
V = \sum (An \cdot Hp \cdot R)
\]
Where:  
- \( A_n \) = negative‑goal activation intensity  
- \( H_p \) = predicted harm to positive goals  
- \( R \) = reversibility factor  

4.3 Override Threshold
\[
V > \theta \Rightarrow \text{Override Detected}
\]
